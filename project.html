  • Places to visit in Amritsar

    1. Golden Temple

      Description :-

      Golden Temple also known as Harimandir sahib (means place where god lives) is spirtually the most significant shrine in sikhism. the man made pool (sarovar) on the site of the temple was completed by the forth sikh guru Shri Guru Ram Das Ji in 1577. It was named as Golden Temple after Maharaja Ranjit Singh Ji added a gold platting to it's beauty which also attracts many tourists across the world. Acoording to many people they get mental piece after visiting there.


    2. Wagah Border

      Description :-

      The border crossing draws its name from Wahga village, near which the Radcliffe Line, the boundary demarcation line dividing India and Pakistan upon the Partition of British India, was drawn. At the time of the independence in 1947, migrants from India entered Pakistan through this border crossing and vice versa. At every evening both the soldiers of India and Pakistan do parade indicating the peace and friendly relation between the two countries.




    3. Durgiana Temple

      Description :-

      Durgiana Mandir is a Hindu temple situated in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. Though a Hindu temple, its architecture is similar to the Sikh Harmandir Sahib . This temple derives its name from the Goddess Durga, the chief Goddess who is worshipped here. Murtis of Lakshmi and Vishnu are also located and worshipped in the temple. it is highly devooted and a great tourist attraction as well.
